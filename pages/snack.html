Snacks

Sanwin makin

Sanwin makin is a dessert that served during festivals or as a street snack. The most popular form of the dessert is known as shwegyi sanwin makin. I really love it!

Mont Lone Yay Paw

Mont lone yay paw, meaning round snack on the water, is a traditional Burmese sweet snack made by combining glutinous rice flour, rice flour, water, and salt. The combination is shaped into smooth balls, which are then typically filled with palm jaggery or palm sugar.

Sago with coconut milk( အုန်းနို့သာကူ )

Sago with coconut milk( အုန်းနို့သာကူ ) is a dessert which is mainly made from coconut and sago. This a editable dessert with different kinds of toppings.

Mont Phet Htok

Mont phet htok is pyramidal rice dumpling filled with coconut meat, which is cooked in sugar or jaggery and then steamed in banana leaves.

Bein Mont

Bein mont is a pancake which is based on rice flour. You put jaggery syrup with coconut shavings and peanuts, sesame seeds.

Khauk mont

Khauk mont is traditinal snack. This snack is a thin and crispy crepe. You make with rice flour and jaggery, and filled with fresh coconut shavings and put red beans as a toppings.

Lan Tha Yel Mote (GANGSTER SNACK)

This snack has a funny name and fresh vegetables are stuffed inside mixing with grand masala, spring onion and chili.

Mont Late Pyar

Mont late pyar (a.k.a butterfly snack), this lady delicately folds these fragile sheets of sticky rice to encase a sweet bean paste before topping with shredded coconut.

Mote Pyar Tha Lat

Mote Pyar Tha Lat is a popular Myanmar Traditional street food. The recipe of this snack is so easy to prepare. Just pour the white mixed paste into a hot crock and add boiled beans and parsley on top of it and it is ready to serve.
